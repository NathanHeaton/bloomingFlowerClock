Snowdrops (Galanthus):

Snowdrops are early spring flowers that bloom after winter, often poking through the snow. Characterized by their delicate, nodding white flowers, they symbolize hope and renewal, bringing a touch of brightness to the winter landscape.

Iris reticulata

Iris reticulata: This dwarf iris blooms in late winter, showcasing vibrant purple, blue, or yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and sunny spots, making it a delightful addition to early spring gardens, often emerging through the last traces of snow.

Primula vulgaris

Primula vulgaris: Commonly known as the primrose, this perennial blooms in early spring with clusters of bright yellow flowers. It prefers partial shade and moist soil, attracting pollinators and adding cheerful color to gardens and woodland edges.

Primula auricula

Primula auricula: Also known as the auricula primrose, this plant features stunning rosettes of colorful flowers in shades of yellow, pink, and purple. Blooming in late spring, it prefers well-drained soil and sunny locations, often found in alpine gardens.

Tulips

Tulips: Iconic spring bulbs, tulips bloom in a rainbow of colors. Their cup-shaped flowers stand tall on sturdy stems, making them popular in gardens and landscapes. They thrive in well-drained soil and full sunlight, symbolizing renewal.

Roses

Roses: Blooming in June, roses are cherished for their exquisite fragrance and variety of colors. From classic reds to vibrant yellows, they thrive in sunny spots and well-drained soil, making them a timeless favorite in gardens worldwide.

Sweet Peas

Sweet Peas: These fragrant climbing plants produce an abundance of colorful. Known for their sweet scent, sweet peas are often used in gardens and cut flower arrangements. They thrive in sunny locations and well-drained soil.

Dahlias

Dahlias: Blooming in late summer, dahlias come in a wide range of colors, shapes, and sizes. They thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making them perfect for vibrant garden displays. Their long-lasting blooms attract pollinators and add bold beauty.

Echinacea

Echinacea: Also known as coneflower, echinacea with striking pink or purple daisy-like flowers. They are hardy perennials that attract butterflies and bees, thriving in sunny locations and well-drained soil, and are often used in herbal remedies.

Nerines

Nerines: Known for their vibrant pink or red flowers , bringing color to the autumn landscape. They thrive in well-drained soil and sunny spots, often found in mixed borders and containers, adding charm to fall gardens.

Camellia

Camellia: Blooming in late autumn to early winter, camellias produce stunning rose-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. These evergreen shrubs thrive in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, adding vibrant color to early winter gardens.

Dogwood

Dogwood: Blooming in late winter, dogwood trees are known for their striking white or pink flowers. Their branches may display vibrant red or yellow stems, adding color to winter landscapes. They thrive in well-drained soil and partial shade.
